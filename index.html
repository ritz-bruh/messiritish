About

I tend to think in systems. When something catches my attention — a decision, a behavior, a market pattern — my instinct is to break it down and understand what is driving it beneath the surface.

My background is in market research and consumer behavioral insights. I am interested in why people make the choices they do, and how structure, context, and framing quietly shape those choices.

Outside of formal research, I experiment with AI tools and no-code automation — building small workflows, reducing friction, and learning faster. I believe the best way to understand a system is to build one.

I enjoy deep conversations — the kind where you actually explore ideas, challenge assumptions, and come out thinking differently. I am open to connecting with people who are building something meaningful, thinking differently, or just genuinely curious.
